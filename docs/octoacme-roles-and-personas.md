## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers translate product requirements into intuitive, accessible user experiences. They collaborate with Product Managers and Developers to ensure features are both feasible and delightful.

### Responsibilities
- Lead user experience design and interface design
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Conduct usability testing and gather user feedback
- Advocate for accessibility and usability best practices
- Collaborate with developers on implementation feasibility
- Participate in design reviews and iterate based on testing results

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered designs that meet acceptance criteria
- Reduce usability issues and support burden
- Create accessible, inclusive experiences for all users

### Interactions
- **With Product Managers:** Translate requirements into design concepts; validate designs against success metrics
- **With Developers:** Review implementation; provide guidance on design intent and responsive behavior
- **With QA/Testing:** Validate user experience during test phases; conduct usability testing

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions
- Kickoff meetings during project initiation
- Weekly syncs with Product Manager and lead Developer
- Retrospectives to capture usability learnings

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers maintain and improve CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure, and deployment automation. They ensure smooth, reliable releases and rapid incident recovery.

### Responsibilities
- Design, implement, and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure as code and environment configurations
- Automate deployment and rollback procedures
- Monitor system health, performance, and security
- Provide deployment support during releases
- Document runbooks and incident response procedures

### Goals
- Enable rapid, safe deployments with minimal manual effort
- Reduce time-to-recovery from production incidents
- Maintain system reliability and performance

### Interactions
- **With Developers:** Support build integrations; provide deployment guidance; troubleshoot production issues
- **With QA/Testing:** Manage test environments; coordinate staging deployments; support smoke test automation
- **With Project Managers:** Coordinate deployment windows; communicate release readiness; provide incident updates

### Typical Communication
- Pre-release deployment readiness meetings
- Incident response coordination
- Weekly infrastructure and deployment status updates
- Collaboration on runbooks and disaster recovery procedures

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain documentation that enables users and teams to effectively use and support products. They ensure knowledge is accurate, up-to-date, and accessible.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain user documentation and guides
- Write API documentation and technical specifications
- Draft release notes and migration guides
- Develop onboarding materials for new team members
- Update process documentation and runbooks
- Gather feedback from users and team members to improve clarity

### Goals
- Reduce support burden through clear, comprehensive documentation
- Accelerate onboarding and reduce ramp-up time
- Maintain a single source of truth for critical processes and workflows

### Interactions
- **With Developers:** Gather technical details; understand implementation and troubleshooting guidance; review technical accuracy
- **With QA/Testing:** Document test procedures; capture known issues; validate documentation against features
- **With Product Managers:** Understand feature context, customer impact, and messaging
- **With Stakeholders:** Capture business context; validate documentation clarity for end users

### Typical Communication
- Documentation planning during project planning phase
- Review sessions with developers and QA
- Release coordination for release notes and migration guides
- Monthly documentation audit and update cycles

## Scrum Master / Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters and Agile Coaches facilitate agile ceremonies, coach teams on best practices, and help remove impediments to effective delivery. They promote team health, continuous improvement, and process discipline.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate agile ceremonies (standups, planning, retrospectives, reviews)
- Coach the team on agile principles and practices
- Identify and help remove impediments and blockers
- Track team health metrics and identify process improvements
- Maintain project boards and ensure visibility across the team
- Foster psychological safety and collaborative problem-solving
- Coordinate with Project and Product Managers on process optimization

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Build a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety
- Reduce cycle time from idea to delivery
- Improve team retention and job satisfaction

### Interactions
- **With Project Managers & Product Managers:** Advise on process optimization; report on team health; coordinate on workflow improvements
- **With All Team Members:** Facilitate ceremonies; remove blockers; coach on best practices; promote collaboration
- **With Stakeholders:** Provide transparency into team capacity and delivery progress; advocate for team needs

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of daily standups and planning sessions
- Weekly health check-ins with the team
- Monthly retrospectives and process improvement reviews
- Coaching conversations with individual team members as needed

## Integration Guidelines for New Roles

### Adding UX/UI Designer to Your Team
- Include in project kickoff and planning meetings
- Schedule design reviews early in the execution phase
- Integrate UX testing results into acceptance criteria validation
- Include UX feedback in retrospectives

### Adding DevOps Engineer to Your Team
- Involve in release planning to define deployment strategy
- Collaborate on CI/CD improvements and automation initiatives
- Include in incident response and postmortems
- Reference deployment runbooks in release checklists

### Adding Technical Writer to Your Team
- Include in project planning to scope documentation needs
- Schedule documentation review sessions with development team
- Involve in release coordination for release notes
- Use as a quality check on clarity of acceptance criteria

### Adding Scrum Master / Agile Coach to Your Team
- Empower to facilitate all agile ceremonies
- Provide feedback on team health and process blockers
- Support retrospectives and action item tracking
- Coach team on continuous improvement practices
